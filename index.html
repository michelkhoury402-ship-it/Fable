Section05

The Core Problem

The technology is defensible. The go-to-market is not.

Fable has no compounding growth mechanism. Every user, viewer, and studio conversation is acquired through one-off effort. A press hit, a viral moment, a personal intro. When the effort stops, growth stops.

The strategic priority: build two self-reinforcing engines. One acquires consumers without paid spend. The other converts consumer traction into enterprise revenue.

Stream One
B2C: Growth Without Spend
Let creators do the marketing. Build a self-sustaining flywheel.
Stream Two
B2B: Revenue Through Licensing
Turn Hollywood IP into Showrunner's unfair advantage.

Section07

B2C: Growth Without Spend

Paid acquisition is closed. Fable doesn't have the budget. It doesn't need to.

Roblox paid creators over $1B in 2025. Those creators produced every piece of content, brought every user, and marketed the platform every time they posted externally. The creators are the marketing.

Showrunner's edge: the creation barrier is near zero. No coding. No animating. Type a prompt, get a finished scene. The creator pool isn't developers. It's everyone.

What infrastructure makes this work in practice:

01
Discovery Feed
Web-based, algorithmically ranked. Best content surfaces regardless of who made it. TikTok proved algorithmic beats follower-based distribution.
02
Real Creator Economics
Viewership rewards on watch metrics. Remix rewards when others build on your IP. Seed 10 to 20 founders. One creator at $5K/month talking publicly beats any campaign.
03
Shared Show Worlds
Pre-built universes with characters, lore, simulation dynamics. Creators drop in, not build from zero. 20 to 30 worlds in 18 months.
04
Creator Progression
Clear ladder. Free creation, then threshold unlocks premium tools, then own show worlds, then Partner with revenue share. 100 views = a win.

The endgame: marketing budget stays near zero. The creator community does acquisition, production, and distribution. The platform's job is to make creators successful. Everything else follows.

Section08

B2B: Revenue Through Licensing

The consumer platform proves the model. Studio licensing monetizes it.

Studio licenses IP to Showrunner. Fable builds a purpose-trained model. Art style, characters, world rules, voices. Fans create and consume within that world. Studio retains ownership of all generated content. Revenue share flows back. Fable gains the IP that makes the platform magnetic.

The demand signal is clear:

Sora shut down March 2026. Disney's $1B deal collapsed. Studios need a new AI partner.
Fan fiction is massive and untapped. Archive of Our Own: millions of users, tens of millions of works. All text. Showrunner gives them video.
Every fan episode shared on TikTok markets both the franchise and the platform. Studios get engagement. Fable gets distribution. Creators get tools.

Section09

Building the Growth Machine

Repeatable systems, not one-off campaigns

Strategy is the easy part. Execution is the job. Everything above means nothing without the operational infrastructure to test, learn, and scale.

Growth comes from fast cycles, not big bets. The core loop: hypothesize, test, measure, scale what works, kill what doesn't. Weekly. No sacred cows.

Speed of learning is the competitive advantage, not budget.

Full-Funnel Ownership

Most marketing teams optimize the top of the funnel. More impressions, more clicks, more signups. That's how you get 100K waitlist signups and 200 active creators. I'd own the full journey:

Awareness
TikTok, YouTube, Meta, Discord. Organic-first with surgical paid amplification on proven creative. Showrunner content IS the ad creative. Native to each platform, not repurposed across all.
Activation
The moment between signup and first scene created. Where most platforms lose 80% of users. Time to first scene under 3 minutes. Pre-loaded worlds. Templated prompts. Every friction point measured.
Retention
Why do people come back on day 7? Day 30? The answer is almost always social. Notifications, community challenges, weekly events, streaks. Cohort analysis from week one. Retention curves by channel, by world, by frequency.
Monetization
Creator subscriptions, credit packs, premium tools. But only after the retention loop is proven. Premature monetization kills growth. The sequence: free value first, habitual usage second, willingness to pay third.

Channel Strategy

Not every channel gets equal weight. Stack-ranked by cost efficiency and feedback speed:

TikTok
Primary organic engine. Showrunner content is natively short-form video. Clip best creator episodes, run organic, boost winners with paid. Test 20+ creatives per week. The algorithm rewards volume and iteration.
YouTube
Long-form proof. Full episodes, creator spotlights, "how I made this" breakdowns. Builds credibility TikTok can't. Best channel for creator recruitment.
Discord
Community, not acquisition. Stop using it as the product. Inner circle for power creators. Feedback, collaboration, ownership. Retention and advocacy, not onboarding.
Meta / IG
Reels for distribution, Stories for community. Mirror TikTok playbook with platform-native tweaks. Likely skews older and more female.
Emerging
Reddit for niche communities (anime, fan fiction, AI). Threads, Bluesky for early adopters. Low investment, high signal.

Experimentation Framework

Not ad-hoc A/B tests. A proper pipeline: hypothesis backlog ranked by impact and ease, standard experiment template, shared results log the whole team can search. Learning should be institutional, not tribal.

What gets tested:

Onboarding flows Prompt suggestions Notification timing Paywall placement Creative formats CTA copy Show world positioning Referral mechanics

Target: 10+ experiments running at any time. Monthly velocity report shared with Product and leadership.

Measurement Architecture

Can't optimize what you can't measure. First 30 days I'd stand up:

DAC
Daily active creators. Scenes created. Episodes completed.
D7 / D30
Retention by cohort, by channel, by show world.
Share %
Share rate, referral rate, activation rate (signup to first scene).

North star metric: weekly active creators who have published at least one scene. This ties acquisition to activation to retention to monetization. If this number grows, everything else follows.

Viral Loops

The product has natural virality. Every scene is shareable. Every creator wants an audience. My job is to engineer the mechanics that amplify this:

Share to Unlock
Create a scene, share it to earn premium credits. Distribution becomes rewarding.
Collaborative Creation
Invite a friend to co-direct an episode. Social creation drives organic invites.
"Make Your Own" Button
On every piece of content. One click from watching to creating. The viewer-to-creator conversion mechanism.
Creator Referrals
Bring a new creator who publishes, both earn credits. Track chains. Invest in super-spreaders.
